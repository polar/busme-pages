Administration Structure

A Busme! Transit Site has a particular structure that fosters the ability to have its management handled by several people simultaneously. Getting routes drawn and schedules filled can take a lot of time. This task for an entire bus system may be done faster by using a divide and conquer method of that task with several Planners.

The top level management component is called a Deployment. Under a Deployment, there exists a collection of entities called Networks. The Deployment and Network are the main components that comprise the management of a Busme! Transit Site. Their respective definitions an purposes are listed below:

Deployment

To help facilitate management of a Busme! Transit site, the site is divvied up into a a number of top level components called a Deployments.

A Deployment is a grouping entity that comprises the entire routes and schedule that may activated for a particular Busme! Transit Site. When you "activate" a Deployment you "run" the system simulation.

Running a system simulation means that your site is modeling vehicle locations according to your entered schedules. Also, your system is able able to collect locations reported from the Busme! Mobile App. Your Busme! Transit system appears as running on its public facing page.

You maintain Deployments to serve as different schedule designations. For example, you may have deployments entitled "Normal" and "Snow Day", each which contains the routes and schedule for every day, and possibly different routes and/or schedules for and emergency snow service, respectively. This approach allows your Planners to create two valid system definitions, and your Operators to activate the one you need with a couple clicks.

You may also use Deployments as workspaces. For instance, you may be able to activate Deployment "Spring 2015", while simultaneously working on Deployment "Fall 2015". Similarly, you might have Deployments owned by other Planners, such as "Marys Space," "Peters Space,", and then you can either merge them or select and copy different Networks from other Deployments into them.

Deployments are owned by people in the Planner role, when they are created by that person. An Operator may activate a particular deployment, but an Operation may not define it or change it.

To help divvy up the workload even more, a Deployment is comprised of Networks, which are logical divisions of Deployments.

When you create a new Busme! Transit site, you are given one Deployment named "Deployment 1" containing one Network, named "Network 1". These components are created for you as a convenience, so that you may start defining your routes and schedules right away. You may change their names, or delete them.

Network

A Network is a logical division of a Deployment. A Network contains the routes and schedules of one or more bus Routes. You may also copy a Network into another Deployment. Therefore, you may have two or three people working on different Routes in their own Deployment workspaces, and copy those Networks to a Deployment that may be activated.

Networks usually represent some section or collection of Routes. For instance, you may have a Deployment named "Normal" with Networks named "Southbound", "Northside", and "Mall Buses".

Working with Networks also may speed up the "compiling" process in that you may only have to change a few things in your Busme! CSV files for a particular Network and upload them for processing without having to upload to reprocess the entire Deployment.

Consistent Deployment

A Deployment may only be activated if it is "consistent". We claim a Deployment is "consistent" if there are no common routes inside different Networks of the Deployment, as that could cause conflicts. So, if two different Networks within a Deployment contained a Route with the Route Code of "240", the Deployment would be inconsistent, and it will not be able to be simulated or activated.

You may still use a Deployment merely as a workspace, such that it may be inconsistent. You may have a Deployment with many Networks that have routes in common, but, you will not be able to activate it or simulate it.

Active Deployment

An Active Deployment is a Deployment that has been "activated," which means the system is running the simulation for that particular Deployment. Only one Deployment may be activated at a time. When activated, the routes and journeys defined within the Networks of the Deployment are simulated on our backend processors. When a Deployment becomes the Active Deployment the public may see the vehicle icons moving on the screen, and the Busme! Mobile App may reports its GPS location for specific vehicles.

Testing Deployment

A Testing Deployment is a Deployment that has been activated for testing, which means the system is running the simulation for that particular Deployment, only in a testing mode. Only one Deployment may be activated for testing at a time. When activated for testing, the routes and journeys defined within Networks of the Deployment are simulated on our backend processors. The result is that your Planners may only see the vehicle icons moving on the Testing Administration screen. The Busme! Mobile App, when in testing mode may report its GPS location for specific vehicles. The Testing Deployment allows you to test certain aspects of your system, such as accuracy of travel times, etc. without exposing the testing system to the public. It is only available to your Planners.

Simulating a Deployment

You have the ability to simulate a Deployment, which is running the simulation of your system for your Planner/Operators viewing only. You can specify a particular date and time, and your Planner may specify a clock multiplier to run it faster. This approach may give your Planner a quick sense if you your definition of the routes and schedules are correct.

NOTE: A simulation takes up needed resources for your other endeavors such as processing your plan files and running your Active Deployment. You get limited resources for such activities until you upgrade your subscription.
