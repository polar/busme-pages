Instructions for the Busme Android App

The first thing you do with the application, is of course turn it on. As long as you have a network, it will try to contact our servers. You will need your GPS location turned on, as that is required to discover which buses are near you and for the operation of the application.


Once it downloads all the route definitions it displays them with a scrollable menu up top naming the routes.
The main screen always consists of a scrollable menu up top which names the routes and the buses. If there are more than 5 entries in the menu, you can scroll it up and down using a flick of your finger on its part of the screen.

The first screen centers the map on your location. The blue lines are the routes the different buses take. However, they are all mixed up together. So, if you want to know which route is which, you can lightly tap ONCE on its menu entry and it will highlight the route in red.

The blue dot is your location.


The routes with active buses will show up with a green R and the buses are marked with a green arrow on the map below indicating the direction they are going.

If you "LONG PRESS" on a route in the menu, which consists of pressing the menu item for a little over one second, the view will change to having the route centered in the map and will show only that route and the buses on it. The menu will list all the active buses on the route below its route name. If there are no active buses on that route, then only the route name is displayed.


The bus entries will list the scheduled start and end time of the particular bus route on the right side.

At any time, you can find your self by performing a DOUBLE TAP, which is two single taps in quick succession, and the map will center on your location.

 If you SINGLE TAP on a particular bus entry on the Menu, it will highlight particular bus route for that bus, and center on the location of the bus at the current map zoom level.

You may alter the zoom level by pinching or spreading two fingers on the map for greater resolution.

If you alter between double taping on the map and single taping on the buses menu entry you can get a feel about how far you may be from that bus, especially at a closer zoom level.

As with any Google map, you may move around by dragging one finger on the map, and you may zoom in and out by pinching or spreading the screen with two fingers.

If you SINGLE TAP on the bus' green arrow icon that is on the map, then you will be looking at only that bus. The menu above will show only the Route name and the particular bus you selected. The route will be green.

This mode may be convenient if that is the bus you want to catch. You may get to this same mode by performing a LONG PRESS on the bus' menu item.

You may also get to this display mode by performing a "LONG PRESS" on the bus' menu entry.


Another way to select is to SINGLE TAP on a particular route on the map and the selection will limit it self to the routes that go through this particular location. You may have to zoom in to get good enough resolution for the selection. You may keep narrowing your selection down by successively selecting down. Keep in mind that some routes are crowded and you may select routes you didn't think actually when on that route.
 

You can get back to your view mode by touching the ANDROID BACK KEY, of which its location depends on your particular phone, but is usually labelled with a back arrow <-.

Another way to alter selection of your routes with the ANDROID MENU Key. This will bring up a menu button.

The menu button consists of really two menu items for you to use. "Nearby Routes" and "Active Routes". The other two menu items help us out with the active development of the phone and you probably need not concern yourself with them, but they will not hurt your phone in anyway.

Once you select "Nearby Routes" your phone's bus route selection criteria will be in that mode until you change it.




The left shows the "Nearby" menu. You can elect to show only routes that are within a specified distance from your phone's current location as determined by the GPS.

The right shows the "Active Routes" menu. This menu is where you may elect to show only routes with travelling buses on them.

Once you select "Nearby Routes" or "Active Routes" your phone's bus route selection criteria will be in that mode until you explicitly  change it.

And that is it. Keep in mind the updating the bus locations depends heavily on your network connectivity.
